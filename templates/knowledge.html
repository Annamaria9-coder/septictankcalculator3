Knowledge Guide

Welcome to the knowledge guide for septic tank design! Here, you'll learn about the basics of septic tanks, including their purpose, components, and considerations for designing an efficient system.

What is a Septic Tank?

A septic tank is an underground system that treats wastewater from households. It separates waste into three layers: scum (top layer), liquid effluent (middle layer), and sludge (bottom layer).

Types of Septic Tanks

  • 3-Chambered Septic Tank: Used when effluent is absorbed into the soil via a leach field.
  • 4-Chambered Septic Tank: Includes a final chamber for treated water to be reused.

Important Factors

  • Wastewater Flow: Average wastewater flow per person ranges between 50–200 L/day.
  • Retention Time: Retention time is typically 1–2 days to allow for proper separation and treatment.
  • Soil Type: Determines the absorption rate in the leach field:
    • Sandy soils: High percolation rates (100+ L/m²/day).
    • Loam soils: Moderate percolation rates (20–60 L/m²/day).
    • Clay soils: Low percolation rates (5–20 L/m²/day).

Mixed Media Filtration

Mixed media, such as sand, charcoal, and coconut husks, further filter the effluent before it exits the septic tank. Each layer performs a specific function, such as reducing odor or filtering solids.

Environmental Safety

Ensure the septic tank is located at a safe distance from water sources like boreholes to prevent contamination. Groundwater flow direction and depth are critical considerations.
